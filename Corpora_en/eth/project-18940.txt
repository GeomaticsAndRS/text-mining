Foundations of Political Economy In this project, we examine the foundations of political competition and social choice. In this project, we examine the foundations of political competition and social choice. In particular, we are concerned with the following issues: Is public information desirable in social choices? How is political competition affected by two-dimensional informational asymmetries regarding the preferences and competence of candidates? How does non-informative advertising (money burning) impact on political signalling games? How should political campaigns be regulated?
