Title: Existence of Rigid Circle and Sphere Packings for Prescribed Densities It is well known that the densest packing of circles with equal diameters has density s2 = /(2 3). A circle packing is called rigid if every circle is squeezed by at least three other circles such that it cannot be moved without overlapping one of them. In 1960 Meschkowski [?] conjectured that the minimal density of a rigid circle packing was 3 /(12 + 7 3) =0.390674... In 1964 Böröczky disproved this conjecture by constructing a rigid circle packing of density 0. A simple construction based on simple dynamical system tools leads to a new rigid circle packing of density 0. More elaborate versions allow to prove that for any number s [0, s 2 ] there is a rigid circle packing with density s. This result is transferred to dimension 3 : for any number s [0, s 3 ] there is a rigid sphere packing with density s (s3 is the density of the densest sphere packing in dimension 3).
