Study of the structure and dynamics of molecular cations by high resolution photoelectron spectroscopy The high resolution that can be achieved by modern photoelectron spectroscopic techniques can be used to obtain new structural and dynamical information on molecular cations. This project aims at studying cations of importance either in atmospheric chemistry and astrophysics, such as N2 + , O2 + , CO2 + , CH4 + , O3 + , H3 + , CH2 + , NH2 + , C2 H4 + , C2 H2 + , C2 H5 + , ... or in laser technology such as Cl2 + , Ar2 + , Kr2 + , Xe2 + , .... Keywords: Ion spectroscopy, spectroscopy of hydride ions, hydrocarbon ions, rare gas dimer ions, ozone cation. Literature: R. Signorell and F. Merkt, J. Chem. Phys. 109, 9762 (1998) R.
