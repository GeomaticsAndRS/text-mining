Due to rising costs in the health sector, the steady increase in musculoskeletal diseases as well as to antizipierenden lack of Hausärztinnen and general practitioners is discussed to redefine the competences in the healthcare sector. By the introduction of direct access (costs for physiotherapy are taken without a doctor's prescription by the basic health insurance) could save costs in various European countries and relieves the medical profession. The aim of this pilot study is, therefore, in Switzerland the consequences of a supply system with direct access to the physiotherapy treatment costs, patient satisfaction /-inside, the doctors/students and physiotherapists/students to evaluate and to compare with those of the traditional system of medical regulation of Physiotherapeutic treatments. Literature: Schämann, A. (2013). Plea for direct access to physical therapy. ZHAW impact, 13/20.
