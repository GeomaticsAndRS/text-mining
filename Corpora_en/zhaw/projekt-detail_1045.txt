In these professions, a significantly large number of women never take up work in their area of studies or leave after a short time (cf. e.g. the ârevolving door effectâ in the IT sector, Ahuja, 2002, Michie Nelson, 2006). In addition to structural factors (such as the compatibility of family and work) that make a career entry difficult or even impossible, communication plays an important role in these occupational areas. Women tend to appear less assertive (Lukoschat Thurn, 2003), use fewer impression management strategies (Schmid Mast, 2001, 2002a 2002b), are more cohesion-oriented when working in teams (Schmid Mast, 2001, 2002a 2002b) and avoid conflicts (Thimm et al., 2003). The project therefore supports young women in the development of their communicative strategies and communicative behaviour in order for them to be able to âhold their groundâ as well as to advance in male-dominated occupational areas. The support is carried out with two services: a seminar and an online communication platform offering e-development services accompanying the seminar. o The seminar informs about the topics of communication and gender. It offers the opportunity to exchange and reflect experiences and to practise communicative situations. o The online communication platform offers electronic consultations (e-coaching). During half a year after the training, seminar participants can take advantage of six electronic consultations. They can prepare challenging situations and analyse them afterwards. The online community also enables them to exchange experiences with other participants about how to approach and master difficult situations. Access to this service is restricted to seminar participants. o Additionally, the online platform offers information about gender and communication and contains links about the topics. It is publicly accessible and can be used beyond the completion of the project. Up until now, the topic of e-coaching in gender studies has not yet been researched extensively; it will therefore be analysed linguistically during the evaluation process. This will enable insights into electronic consultation processes and into the training of skills in electronic consultation. Moreover, seminar participation and the use of the electronic platform will be evaluated quantitavely and the seminars and the e-coaching will be assessed using questionnaires and interviews. This information can then be used for the development of follow-up programmes.
