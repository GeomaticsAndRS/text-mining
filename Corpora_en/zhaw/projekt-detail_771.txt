The thriving on renewed stocks of wild hoofed animals are in the perception and the interests of the people in a field of tension. The competent cantonal hunting authorities have the challenging task to find a deal with the wild ungulates that allows healthy populations and at the same time protects the interests of the involved ten users groups. This objective requires an efficient, professional wildlife management, which must rely on comprehensive understanding about the wild animal stocks. It is important that the populations in a spatial reference provided and the distribution of favourable habitats is taken into account. The opinion is to link small-scale and resulting political border areas into larger landscape units. The definition of these wild spaces should rely on wild animal ecological criteria. At the same time the wild spaces should have a meaningful managed dimension. The habitats of the deer, the wild boar and the Red to be considered for this Division.
