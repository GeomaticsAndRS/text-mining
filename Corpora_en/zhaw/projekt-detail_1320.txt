SBB - Division passenger services - market research which is continuous movement (KEP) is a long-term study of the Swiss Federal Railways (SBB), which examines the relevant railway mobility behavior of the Swiss resident population since 1983. The collection includes some 2,400 persons per month, or about 28000 people per year, which are surveyed in telephone interviews. It turned out that some, determined by the CEP characteristics over time exhibit trends and fluctuations, which experts seem implausible. This applies in particular to the modal split share train relevant market too, where in addition to increased variability was observed a decrease of passenger transport. The investigation focused on the temporal changes of the interviewees properties. Over the period of the survey resulted in slight shifts, whose Einfluss could be quantified. With a new correction method, freed scores were finally calculated by these adverse influences.
