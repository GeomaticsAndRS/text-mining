The SENPAN study (formerly Swiss-agile-study) is designed as a monitoring study for the detection of the nutritional situation of older people in the German-speaking Switzerland. SENPAN has a modular design and pursuing multiple levels of objectives (main and secondary objectives). Diet plays an important role for the individual health and well-being, as well as the subjective satisfaction of the higher age. Not care, mobile adults with no functional loss from 60 years in the canton of Zurich will be investigated in this study. Target is the usual way of consumption, to collect nutrient supply, on the other hand to investigate the actions and structures of food everyday in different life situations on the one hand.
