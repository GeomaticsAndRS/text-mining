"Culture" is regarded by science as the fourth dimension of sustainability, in addition to the ecological, social and economic aspects. This fourth, cultural dimension that includes the maintenance of cultural identity and diversity as a cornerstone for sustainable landscape and regional development. On this increasingly important and recognized aspect of sustainability, this project mainly aims and focuses on the development of landscape with cultural buildings. The aim of the project is to investigate the research gaps and the need for practical (spatial planning, historic preservation, landscape and cultural heritage protection, regional development) on the handling of the architectural heritage in regem cultural landscape change. It should develop a practical research project and a site analysis finds good case study regions. The project was completed the end of 2013 and in a ZHAW report published in March 2014.
