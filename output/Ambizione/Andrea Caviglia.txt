A major challenge facing condensed-matter physics today is to extend this control to quantum materials with functional properties such as magnetism or superconductivity, and to develop a deeper understanding of their complex behaviour.

An appealing possibility is to selectively excite low-frequency modes of the solid such as lattice vibrations or the more exotic dipole-carrying excitations of condensed matter that range from phason modes of charge density waves to Josephson plasma waves.
