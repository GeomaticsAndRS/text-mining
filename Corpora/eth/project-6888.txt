  High resolution photoelectron spectroscopy - Methodology  

Recent progress in threshold ionization techniques have led to a considerable improvement of the resolution of photoelectron spectroscopy, the best resolution achieved so far (0.06 cm-1 ) being sufficient to determine details of the energy level structure of molecular ions. The best resolution is achieved by selectively ionizing Rydberg states of very high principal quantum number (n > 250) located just below each energy level of molecular ions. This project is devoted to fundamental studies of the field ionization behaviour of high Rydberg states and aims at improving the selectivity of the field ionization process and thus go beyond the current resolution limit of photoelectron spectroscopy. We are also trying to resolve the Rydberg state structure at very high n values and combine PFI-ZEKE photoelectron spectroscopy with Rydberg series extrapolation to determine the hyperfine structure of cations.
