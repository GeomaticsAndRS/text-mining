  Basic Research and Growth Policy  
We examine the role of (public) basic research on long-run economic  growth in order to derive optimal policies. Main fields: A country's optimal amount of basic research expenditure in a globalized world? The optimal financing scheme for it? On which economic sectors should one concentrate?
In this project, we examine the role of (public) basic research on long-run economic growth with the aim to derive optimal policies. The main research questions are: What is a country's optimal amount of public basic research expenditure in a globalized world? What is the optimal fincancing scheme for basic research? Should basic research efforts be concentrated on certain economic sectors?
