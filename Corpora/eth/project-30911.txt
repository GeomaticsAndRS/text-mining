The model of a democracy that conform to the (e.g. financial) market distributes responsibilities for losses unfairly. It is not more intelligent, because price signals [from the market] as a basis for determining policies are too diffuse, too selective and lacking of content. Reaching consensus in a democracy depends on more diverse aspects than those that are relevant in negotiating between market actors.
