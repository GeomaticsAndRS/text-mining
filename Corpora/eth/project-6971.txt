  Examination stress of college students  

 Examination stress of college students is investigated in two different test situations and compared with a control situation, taking part within four days. The first test situation consists of a written examination and the marks given account to the final degree. The second test situation contains the Raven Matrix intelligence test, its results do not count for the final degree. In the control situation, the students are attending a lecture. The study consists of two parts. In the first part the physiological variable cortisol was assessed from saliva samples. In the second part heart rate, respiration and physical activity from continuous 24-hour recordings were assessed. Psychological variables as test anxiety, personality factors and coping behavior are assessed with questionnaires and compared with the physiological measurements and the test results.
